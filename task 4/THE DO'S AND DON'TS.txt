The 5 Do's of Social Media
DO audit your privacy settings. Regularly check who can see your posts and personal information on platforms like Instagram and Facebook.
DO think before you post. Ask yourself if you would be comfortable with an internship coordinator or your family seeing the photo or comment.
DO use platforms for networking. Use LinkedIn to connect with alumni, join industry groups, and showcase your academic projects.
DO respect others' privacy. Always ask for permission before posting photos of your batchmates or tagging them in your content.
DO verify information before sharing. Fact-check news articles or sensational posts to ensure you aren't spreading misinformation to your peers.
 The 5 Don'ts of Social Media
DON'T complain about professors or employers. Venting online leaves a permanent, public footprint that can harm your academic or professional reputation.
DON'T share your exact location or schedule. Avoid posting real-time updates of where you are or your daily class route for safety reasons.
DON'T post evidence of illegal or irresponsible behavior. Photos involving underage drinking or substance abuse can lead to university disciplinary action or lost job offers.
DON'T engage in internet flame wars. Getting into heated, aggressive arguments in comment sections is unprofessional and rarely productive.
DON'T use your university email for personal accounts. Keep your academic/professional accounts separate from your casual social media profiles.
